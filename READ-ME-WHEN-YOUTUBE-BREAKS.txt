==============================================================================
  YouTube says "Sign in to confirm you're not a bot" again?
  Your cookies.txt expired. Here's how to fix it. Takes ~3 minutes.
==============================================================================

WHY THIS HAPPENS
----------------
YouTube needs proof you're a logged-in human. We give it that with a
"cookies.txt" file exported from a browser where you're signed in to YouTube.

Google rotates its login sessions every few days/weeks, which quietly kills
the old cookies.txt. When that happens, downloads start failing again with
the "Sign in to confirm you're not a bot" error. The fix is always the same:
EXPORT A FRESH cookies.txt. Nothing is broken — the cookies just got old.


THE FIX (step by step)
----------------------
1. Open a PRIVATE / INCOGNITO window in your browser.
      - Chrome/Edge:  Ctrl+Shift+N
      - Firefox:      Ctrl+Shift+P
   (Private window matters — see "WHY INCOGNITO" at the bottom.)

2. Go to  youtube.com  and SIGN IN. Make sure your avatar shows in the
   top-right corner. If you're not actually signed in, the export will be
   useless (it'll have no login in it).

3. With that YouTube tab still open, click your cookies-export extension and
   choose Export / Save. Recommended extension:
      "Get cookies.txt LOCALLY"   (Chrome/Edge)
      "cookies.txt"               (Firefox)
   Export the cookies for youtube.com.

4. Save / rename the exported file to exactly:   cookies.txt
   and put it in THIS folder (replace the old one):

      C:\Users\jnlco\OneDrive\Documents\Projects\Media Downloader\cookies.txt

5. CLOSE the incognito window right away (don't keep browsing in it).

6. Restart the app (close it and run run.bat again) and try a download.


VERIFY IT WORKED (optional but recommended)
-------------------------------------------
Open PowerShell in this folder and run this one line. It checks the new file
actually contains a login:

   .venv\Scripts\python.exe -c "d=open('cookies.txt',encoding='utf-8').read(); print('LOGGED IN - good to go' if any(k in d for k in ('__Secure-3PSID','__Secure-1PSID','LOGIN_INFO')) else 'STILL LOGGED OUT - re-export, you were not signed in')"

   - "LOGGED IN - good to go"     -> you're done.
   - "STILL LOGGED OUT ..."       -> you weren't actually signed in at step 2.
                                     Redo it and make sure your avatar shows.


IF IT STILL FAILS
-----------------
- Chrome/Edge exports on Windows sometimes come out logged-out even when you
  ARE signed in (their cookie encryption). If that keeps happening, use
  FIREFOX instead: sign in to YouTube in a Firefox private window and export
  from there. Firefox is the most reliable on this machine.

- A specific video that says "login required" / age-restricted / members-only
  will ALWAYS need a logged-in cookies.txt — that's expected, not a bug.

- If even fresh cookies fail on many videos, update the engines, then retry:
     .venv\Scripts\python.exe -m pip install -U yt-dlp gallery-dl


MAKE THE COOKIES LAST LONGER
----------------------------
The cookies live longest if you DON'T reuse that login for normal browsing.
Best practice: use a private window (or a throwaway Google account) ONLY to
export, then close it. Browsing YouTube normally in that same account makes
Google rotate the session and expire your exported file sooner.


WHY INCOGNITO?
--------------
A private window gives a fresh, isolated login session. Because you close it
immediately after exporting, Google doesn't "rotate" (and invalidate) those
cookies the way it would if you kept using that login in your everyday
browser. Result: the exported cookies.txt stays valid much longer.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TL;DR: Incognito -> sign in to youtube.com -> export cookies -> save as
cookies.txt in this folder -> close incognito -> restart the app.
